Space Environment and Its Challenges

  • Mahir Yadav

Space environment is a branch of astronautics, aerospace engineering and space physics which tries to understand and respond to the conditions existing in space that affect the design, construction and operation of a spacecraft. This also helps in ensuring safe passage of astronauts while on space missions. The effects on spacecraft arises mainly from outer space radiations, space debris, asteroids, meteoroids, friction, exposure to charged particles, etc. Space environments also refers to the environment that is present in the space.

The sources of radiations in space primarily include the Van Allen radiation belts, Solar Energetic Particles, Galactic Cosmic Rays. A Van Allen radiation belt is a zone of energetic charged particles, most of which originate from the solar wind, that are captured by and held around a planet by that planet's magnetic field. Earth has two such belts. Solar energetic particles are high-energy particles coming from the sun. They consist of protons and electrons which have high levels of energy. Cosmic rays are high-energy protons and atomic nuclei which move through space at nearly the speed of light. They can originate from the Sun or from outside of the solar system and/or from distant galaxies. These radiations can severely damage the electronic components and solar cells.

Space debris and meteoroids can impact spacecraft at high speeds, causing mechanical or electrical damage. The average speed of space debris is 10 km/s while the average speed of meteoroids is much greater. Thousands of space debris are orbiting Earth at great speeds. The fragments as small as quarter of inch can cause immense damage to space crafts. Mechanical damage from debris impacts have been studied through space missions including LDEF (Long Duration Exposure Facility) by NASA which had over 20,000 documented impacts through its 5.7-year mission.

Spacecraft electrostatic charging is caused by the hot plasma environment around the Earth. The plasma encountered in the region of the geostationary orbit becomes heated during geomagnetic substorms caused by disturbance in the solar wind, Hot electrons collect on surfaces of spacecraft and can establish electrostatic potentials of the order of kilovolts. As a result, discharges can occur and are known to be the source of many spacecraft abnormalities.

With these there are many other challenges that a spacecraft faces when in space. These challenges are centred on three key themes: (1) Expand human presence in space, (2) Manage in-space resources, and (3) Enable transformational space exploration and scientific discovery. The challenges include space health and medicine, space colonization, affordable abundant power and surviving extreme space environment.

Space is an extreme environment that is not suitable for human beings. In order to live and effectively work in space for an extended the period of time, people require technologies that enable survival in extreme environment, comprehensive space-based physiological and physical health management and comprehensive medical care in a limited infrastructure. Currently, the infrastructure and integrated technologies needed to enable permanent, self-sufficient human settlements away from Earth do not exist. Effective systems do not exist to replenish consumable resources. This makes long term stays cost-prohibitive and poses significant risk to personnel if resupply missions do not arrive on time.

Mass and lifetime considerations of today's space power systems limit our missions. Current spacecraft power systems degrade over time, thereby reducing the amount of power available for use as the mission progresses. Space harbours extreme environments like extreme heat and cold, ultra-vacuum, gravitational value atomic oxygen, and high energy radiation, and all these affect machine operations and survival. To accomplish the goal of exploring a wide range of targets across our solar system requires the ability to survive extreme space environments.

Space environment affect the spacecraft through apart from others, microgravity, vacuum, and extreme temperature. The hard vacuum of space can cause deposits on the spacecraft's surface. This molecular contamination can affect the optical properties of a vehicle and payload surfaces and spacecraft performance, especially for sensitive optics. The extreme temperature may lead to cracking, or formation of pinholes in the coating and/or surface material of the spacecraft.

The challenges of space environment will require truly radical improvement in all technologies to overcome it. The space environment challenges are not restricted to what spacecraft and astronauts encounter when they travel in space, but also it includes the asteroids, meteoroids and comets hurling at high speeds through the space and have a possibility of striking Earth.

Therefore, it is critical for man to pursue deeper understanding of space environment so that he can learn more, harness space for growth and development of mankind and finally enable himself to protect this beautiful earth and life on it from extinction, or otherwise may meet the same fate that millions of years back dinosaurs faced in a meteor strike and perished. Therefore, it is important for mankind to continue expanding his boundaries in space while meeting its challenges technically and intelligently.
